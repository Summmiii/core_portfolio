Parsons School of Design, known colloquially as Parsons, is a private art and design college located in the Greenwich Village neighborhood of Lower Manhattan in New York City. It is one of the five colleges of The New School. The school was founded in 1896 by William Merritt Chase in search of individualistic artistic expression. It was the first of its kind in the country to offer programs in fashion design, advertising, interior design, and graphic design. The school offers undergraduate and graduate programs ranging from architectural design, curatorial studies, to textiles and design and urban ecologies. Among Parsons alumni are many famous fashion designers, photographers, designers, illustrators, and artists who have made significant contributions to their respective fields. The college is a member of the National Association of Schools of Art and Design and the Association of Independent Colleges of Art and Design.
In the ongoing series “One of These Black Boys” (images 2 and 3), alumnus Tariku Shiferaw (@abstractexit, MFA Fine Arts ‘15), interrogates the act of mark-making in society, highlighting the power struggles between those who are allowed to make marks and those who get marked. Using music from the African diaspora as titles for his works, Shiferaw’s art inherits the references, identities, and histories portrayed through the songs. This is a snapshot from the residency of Sheila Bridges, AAS Interior Design graduate and industry changemaker. Bridges, who was named “America’s Best Interior Designer” by @cnn and @time magazine, founded her own interior design firm in 1994. She has thoughtfully designed residences and offices for prominent entertainers, entrepreneurs, and business professionals. Bridges cites persistence as a key to success, and hopes her story will inspire young designers of color to courageously follow their dreams as well.
